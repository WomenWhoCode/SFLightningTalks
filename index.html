Women Who Code SF

We are a non-profit dedicated to inspiring and supporting women to excel in technology careers!

We hold events and foster community for women embarking on tech careers or growing their careers. We are part of the larger Women Who Code, find out more about our network and WWCode Global.

The easiest way to find out about upcoming events is to join our Meetup group!

Join Us on Slack

Come hang out from the comfort of your computer! We strive to be a supportive community and so we expect respect and understanding from all of our members. We expect everyone to read and abide by our Code of Conduct. Fill this out and we'll get you an invite! (Then look underneath the form to see more about how our slack works)

Code of Conduct

WWCode events are intended for all women (trans and cis), trans men, and genderqueer folks who are interested in attending. We are emphatically queer and trans friendly and committed to matters of social justice as an organization. Select events may be open to the general public, which will be clearly noted in event descriptions. If you have any questions (or identify differently and want to attend), please contact the directors of this network.

Read the full Code of Conduct on Github. It applies to in-person events as well as in slack and any other community spaces.

We will prioritize the well-being and safety of recipients of a violation over the comfort of the violator. "Good intentions" do not diminish the effects of an act.

Reporting and Enforcement

There are several ways to report a CoC violation, please choose the one that makes the most sense.

  • Inform the event Lead: Best for immediate action at an event
  • Use the /admin command: Sends a message to all the admins on Slack, best for Slack-related violations
  • Email the directors: The directors are also admins
  • Use this anonymous form: This goes to WWCode Global, no one in the SF network has access to it
Any reported information will only be shared internally as necessary as part of resolving the report. Every code of conduct violation report will be treated with seriousness and care.
